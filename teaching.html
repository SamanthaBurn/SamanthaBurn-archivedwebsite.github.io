Teaching

I served as a Teaching Fellow in the following Harvard University courses.

ECON 2020B: Microeconomic Theory II

Instructor: Christopher Avery (Spring 2018, 2019)

This course is part of the Harvard Kennedy School core microeconomics sequence for doctoral students. Topics include game theory, economics of information, incentive theory, and welfare economics. I was awarded the Derek Bok certificate of distinction and excellence in teaching in 2018 and 2019.

ECON 1460: The Economics of Health Care Policy

Instructor: Joseph Newhouse (Fall 2018)

This course is an introduction to health economics and policy for Harvard Kennedy School Master's MPP and MPA students, Harvard School of Public Health MPH and doctoral students and advanced undergraduates. Topics include the demand for medical care services, the demand for insurance, payment of physicians and hospitals, and health care reform.

I also served as a volunteer teacher at the Harvard School of Public Health Youth and Public Health conference, designed to help Boston high school students from diverse backgrounds learn about the field of public health.
